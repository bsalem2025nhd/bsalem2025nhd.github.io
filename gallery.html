Explore a selection of images picked straight from the Revolution.



Carrying the Nobility

This political cartoon depicts a poor man from the Third Estate breaking his back by carrying the nobles and clergy.

Storming the Bastille

This is a painting of the famous 1789 storming of the royal Bastille prison.


Tennis Court Oath

This iconic drawing depicts the Tennis Court Oath where members of the National Assembly vowed not to leave until France had a constitution.


Letter from the King

This is a letter written by none other than King Louis XIV, the monarch at the time of the Revolution.


Death of Marat

This famous painting shows Jean-Paul Marat dead in his bathtub after being murdered by Charlotte Corday. The radical writer was later martyrized by the French people.


Abolition of Privileges

This image depicts the National Assembly abolishing the nobility's privilege to be tax-exempt among others on August 4, 1789.


Ordinary Guillotine

This is a drawing of a guillotine, the mechanism used during the revolution used to execute everyone from peasants to the king.


Robespierre Gets Sliced

This drawing depicts Maximilien Robespierre getting a taste of his own medicine: he got executed by guillotine, the method he killed so many commoners with.


Marie Antoinette's Execution

A large crowd gathers round to watch the former queen of France get beheaded.


Mass Murder

These nine people were part of the almost 17,000 people to die to the guillotine during the Revolution.
